Life As A Stream

Time is like a river. We are flowing in that. Sometimes another stream comes and meets us. But unlike an actual river, they don't dissolve or melt away into us, rather they flow alongside.

Apr 09, 2018 • Arnab Das • #100-words

Time is like a river. We are flowing in that. Sometimes another stream comes and meets us. But unlike an actual river, they don’t dissolve or melt away into us, rather they flow alongside. And may be at a later point in time, they cleave themselves to take another route. But the time, we are together, mostly we both benefit exchange of views, helping each other and mostly being harmony. Yes, the last one is the most important, be connected, be one with other. This is required to flow. There may come some rocks, some perils of our stream are blocked. But with someone, in unison, we can flow over the rocks, boulders or fear of being obstructed. Alone we may not, but together we can come along any hindrance if the partner is right. Yes. This is another important aspect. Choosing the right or appropriate partner to flow contiguously. If we are not wise enough to choose the right one, the other flow may water down our own flow. Then the harmony will be missing. We will not be able to benefit from being together. So, we have to be cautious in whom we are choosing to flow along with us.
