About Love

Love. One of the very few words which has it's equivalent in all languages. And we can't deny whatever macabre acts have hitherto been done in the name of Love, it has always delighted anyone, in the mere prospect of get some.

Sep 11, 2017 • Arnab Das • #100-words

Today I’m quite curious about a topic, that has always intrigued people generations after generations, irrespective of their creed, beliefs whatever. Love. One of the very few words which has it’s equivalent in all languages. And as far as have seen, the Love word in every languages sounds very sweet. And we can’t deny whatever macabre acts have hitherto been done in the name of Love, it has always delighted anyone, in the mere prospect of get some. Really don’t know, what is love. But I love to be in the feelings, that have been developed over time for this word. Everyone has different feelings for this word. And what I love, you may not. So, what for me is a lovely atmosphere, you may laugh or joke on. But you can’t deny that is not inducing some sort of soothing feelings. Yes. Though each one of us bears different feelings for the same word, those feelings have a common base. Common ground, from which they all have been constructed brick by brick. People are very choosy in filling the box of feelings for love. In that common ground we can all gather together to be united with others. To be one. There would be no difference in beliefs, in doings. I really doubt, the thoughts of falling in love for the first time in a girl from India is different from one from Israel or US or around the world. May be the context, country, language, education, society is different, but first feelings are the last feelings, lasts forever in every consequent falling in love, breaking relationship and when going to die. And people don’t feel content if the first feeling does not felt in any later relations. Lot of people makes multiple relations, probably they just search for their first love, the depth, the unknown feelings in heart, mind, body. Some controls themselves. But every one likes to feel the first love always. In Indian scriptures for sages, it has been advised to channel the Love for nature, food and bhajan(prayer). Even the iron clad mind of a moralistic saint couldn’t deny the need of Love. And some sects actually try to feel love in the love of Purush, God and Prakriti, all else, symbolised as Krshna and Radha, the eternal love affair between two teens a long time ago. Sages through generations have thrilled, have been engulfed in the purest Love affair written ever. In it’s purest form, body can’t be differenciated from mind. So true Love does not discard body, rather embraces it to be more close and united. Today don’t want to discuss about the consequences of it. But we should confine ourselves in our humane nature, to unify with every one else, to expand the horizon, to embrace Prakriti.
