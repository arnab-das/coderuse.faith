Musical Instruments And Me

Sep 09, 2017 • Arnab Das • #100-words

I love to listen to music. I do not understand the grammar of music very much. Only listen what sounds good to my ear. Currently, I’m watching the NCIS series. There Abby Sciuto, the allrounder forensic analyst, after disclosing she maintains a NAS to keep all the music, she usually listens to, asks the war veteran, multiple times purple heart winner Special Agent Gibbs, how many songs he usually listens. He promptly answers five. She gasped only five thousand. He just informatively answered no just five. Almost the same applies for me. I do not remember the lyrics of a whole song. Only some parts, that strikes. And probably I listen to only twenty to thirty songs if I have to select what I’ve listened to. When I do daily essential domestic chores, generally select a playlist in Gaana and that plays indefinitely until I finish the work. That time, some sound has to linger in the empty rooms. Just that. When my mother lives with me. Then I don’t require to play the Gaana playlist. She talks with me continuously.

A long time ago, I invested some time to learn vocal music. Later had to suspend that, due to the bad condition of the throat. At that time I used to play Harmonium. A very popular instrument in our country for vocal musicians. Some vocalists also practice with Tanpura. But for the new learners, teachers recommend Harmonium, as it’s hassle free. And gives the correct tone and pitch without any trouble. With Tanpura, one has to configure it properly before starting rewaz, the practice. So, Tanpura is not generally given before five or six years’ students or serious ones. Harmonium is an interesting instrument. There are a lot of Harmonium playing musicians also. They are quite amazing in crafting tricky tunes in a very fast pace. A lot like pianists.

After a long time leaving Harmonium started to learn Violin. It’s regarded as the most difficult learn instruments among all. I like the contiguous tone of it. And the melody, that can be made, is amazing. Only it needs a lot of regular practice and patience to play well. And I don’t have the reputation of being a very attentive student. I like to play the violin. But as any other things, my internal mechanism opposes me to have anything as a habit, except bathing and eating. Currently, I’m not practicing it daily. Hope that some day I’ll be able to and some day I’ll be able to play violin nicely.
