Anonymous Shakespear

And truthfully, this play of mucking the name "Shakespear" is quite distasteful and proofs are mostly circumstantial.

Oct 30, 2017 • Arnab Das • #100-words

Yesterday was watching “Anonymous”. Before this never knew of the Oxfordian Theory Of Shakespear Authorship. And truthfully, this play of mucking the name “Shakespear” is quite distasteful and proofs are mostly circumstantial. No doubt the earlier works, attributed to Shakespear are much more rich in literary excellence than the later ones. But we should not forget the fact that, Shakespear became the most popular, successful and hence one of the busiest playwrights of his time. Great work needs a great deal of time and patience. I think the downfall of quality and richness of creations are expected, as we see in the works of literary geniuses in modern days also. I’ve found only two facts that may go against the authenticity as the writer for the man known as Shakespear.

The fact that, there are no manuscripts written by Shakespear, may happen if the creator dictates his creation to scriptors and of-course at the same time, more than one copy can be made, and that may be required. He was a busy man. So, it was really convenient. But earlier days. Shouldn’t at least some drafts or final copies for the earlier creations be found? Whatever may be the fact, it’s hard to believe, that a playwright won’t relish writing his own piece for a single time. And if we want to believe that, every time he used to let someone copy his manuscript before giving someone, then also at least some specimen should there be to read. Afterall it’s only 400 years ago. And belongings of someone of his stature and popularity could have been well preserved by at-least the fans, if not by him. And also it’s hard to believe, such a romantic individual couldn’t scratch at least some words to give his fans, friends or well-wishers.

And the second doubt is, the plays exhibit an immense knowledge about courts, not only of England but also French and Italia at that time, from the author. And there is no such evidence that can prove Shakespear’s adjacency to such an elite society in his life, to equip him to depict the orders and atmosphere with such an elegance.

These are two discrepancies I’ve found to be hard to believe. Otherwise, in a way, this kind of discussions are of no use. For a long time those plays, poems, literary works are known to be written by Shakespear. Even if they are not actually, they are by common beliefs. And Edward De Vere was not just a vagrant to be imposed upon. Most of the plays were published in his lifetime. If he suppressed his identity, we should respect him. And try not to mucker a name, worshipped by all, who knows at least the name of the language “English” and disrespect the wish of a noble man.
